# Site Design Philosophy

## Thesis

The Comic Book Museum should feel like a curated exhibition rather than a fan site, blog, or slideshow. Every page should behave like a room, every image should behave like an object, and every supporting surface should deepen the visitor's understanding of Marvel as a historical and cultural system.

## Governing Metaphor

This project uses the metaphor of a museum exhibition catalog.

That metaphor creates five responsibilities:

1. the visitor must feel guided rather than dumped into flat navigation
2. chronology must act as the main route through the subject
3. companion pages must behave like supporting rooms, not random extras
4. images must function as evidence, artifacts, or atmosphere
5. restraint must matter more than decoration

## Curatorial Logic

### 1. The chronology spine comes first

The exhibition is anchored by three chronology rooms:

- Golden Age Origins
- Marvel Age Expansion
- Crossover and Cinematic Age

These rooms carry the main argument. Other pages are subordinate to them.

### 2. Companion rooms deepen the argument

The supporting pages exist because visitors need different kinds of evidence:

- publication lineage for issue-based proof
- people and studios for cast and institution logic
- object gallery for concentrated visual comparison

These pages must strengthen the chronology, not compete with it.

### 3. Objects must be visible

The site should not only mention artifacts. It should stage them. A comic, prop wall, editorial desk, or portrait card should read like an exhibition object with a title, role, and relation to the broader story.

### 4. The tone should balance seriousness and excitement

Marvel is energetic, but the exhibition should still feel disciplined. The project therefore uses Bauhaus influence for hierarchy, spacing, typography, and restraint while allowing Marvel content to supply energy and recognizability.

## What Must Stay True In Future Changes

- the chronology remains the primary visitor route
- companion rooms remain clearly subordinate to the chronology
- object images are used as evidence rather than filler
- cast pages group actors by curatorial importance, not alphabet alone
- visual consistency is treated as part of curatorial credibility
- new additions must strengthen the museum thesis rather than add clutter

## Anti-Drift Guidance

Do not let the site drift into:

- a general Marvel encyclopedia
- a comic-shop style product grid
- a blog-like stream of unrelated content
- a process diary about how the site was made

The project succeeds when it feels like a coherent exhibition argument with visible artifacts and disciplined structure.
